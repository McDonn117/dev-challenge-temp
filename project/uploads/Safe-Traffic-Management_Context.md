## 6. SEO and keyword strategy

**Geographic focus:** Queensland first (nail the home market), Brisbane the priority market, then Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast and Northern NSW. Potential to expand further later. Kurt floated "nationwide" but the agreed focus is Queensland to start.

**Indicative keyword volumes from the keyword research** (approximate, drawn from the sales transcript, validate against the actual keyword data file):
- Traffic controllers (Brisbane): ~323/mo
- Traffic management, Gold Coast: ~30/mo
- Traffic management, Sunshine Coast: ~10/mo
- Long-form authority topics: roughly ~20 searches/mo each

**Strategic note / risk to flag:** Kurt has contacts in WA who run a traffic control company. Their advice was that SEO will not be the main channel for winning traffic control work, that work comes through relationships and their operating model rather than search. This contrasts with KLB, where search visibility matters more. We still see opportunity in both, but for STM specifically we should set realistic expectations about SEO's role and lean on reporting to prove the lead value. Google Ads (see Commercial) may be a faster lead lever for STM early on.

Method reminder for the team: it is not about the website, it is about the outcomes it generates. Keyword data informs the site structure. Engineer for both the search engine and the user. Strong SEO also correlates with discoverability in AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude), which is increasingly how prospects find providers.
